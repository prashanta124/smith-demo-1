About

Researcher of trust, cognition, and AI systems

Magdalena Wischnewski is a researcher in psychology, media psychology, and human-AI interaction. Her work examines how people understand, trust, evaluate, and rely on artificial intelligence systems, especially in contexts where technology, uncertainty, communication, and social identity intersect.

Her academic background includes a PhD in Psychology from the University of Duisburg-Essen, completed summa cum laude, with a thesis on misinformation on social media and motivated reasoning. Her research has developed across AI trust, misinformation, explainable AI, AI certification, anthropomorphism, and the social perception of automated systems.

Research Areas

Core themes

Trust in AI

Understanding how users calibrate trust in automated systems, certifications, AI labels, system performance, and explainable AI.

Misinformation & Motivated Reasoning

Studying how identity, emotion, belief, and prior opinion shape people’s engagement with misinformation and polarizing news.

Human-AI Communication

Investigating how language, mentalistic framing, anthropomorphism, and public communication affect perceptions of AI and LLMs.

Trustworthy AI Systems

Examining how AI certification, governance, labels, and user-facing communication can support responsible AI development.
